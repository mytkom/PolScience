Notka biograficzna

Brak danych

am an academic lecturer and trainer affiliated with the Faculty of Engineering Management at Bialystok University of Technology. My research area is positioned within Management and Quality sciences and focuses on human resource management, employer branding, and evidence-based people practices. In my doctoral research, I examine corporate family-friendly policies as a strategic lever for shaping employer branding and strengthening organisational attractiveness in the labour market. I am particularly interested in how pro-family solutions influence employees’ perceptions, engagement, and retention, with special attention to working parents and the mechanisms behind turnover intentions.

My research interests include family-friendly and caregiver-support practices, employee well-being and mental health at work, sustainable and responsible HRM, talent attraction and retention, and the HR dimension of ESG and DEI initiatives. I also explore how organisational culture, leadership behaviours, and internal communication translate formal policies into real employee experience, and how these practices can be measured and managed as part of quality-oriented organisational systems.

I am a co-creator and Deputy Head of the postgraduate programme “Psychology in Management” at my faculty, where I contribute to curriculum design and the development of practice-oriented learning paths that integrate management knowledge with psychological insights.

I actively seek opportunities for international academic collaboration, including joint research projects, comparative studies, and co-authored publications. I am particularly interested in partnering with scholars and institutions working on HRM, employer branding, well-being, and responsible management in diverse cultural and organisational contexts.

For my teaching and educational leadership, I was honoured with the Best Lecturer (Najlepszy Dydaktyk) award in 2023 and 2024.
