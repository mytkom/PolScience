Notka biograficzna

Marcin Butlewski's field of interest is connected with Ergonomics, Occupational Safety,

and Human Factors Engineering. Research varies a wide range of applications,

from the ergonomic assessment of working environments to the design of tools

and systems for individuals with disabilities. Especially the following areas

of research can be distinguished: 1. Ergonomic Assessment and Design - the

research area encompasses the evaluation of work environments and the creation

of ergonomic tools and systems aimed at enhancing workplace safety and

efficiency. It involves developing quality improvement models for small and

medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) to take advantage of ergonomics and implement

it into managerial processes. 2. Occupational Health and Safety - with the

special emphasis to the psychophysical capabilities of workers, the role of

fatigue in safety systems, and the development of strategies to mitigate human

error within occupational practices. 3. Universal and Inclusive Design - with

the focus on the development of design solutions for a diverse range of users,

particularly the aging population and those with disabilities, utilizing

universal design principles and innovative methodologies such as TRIZ and

Zwicky's morphological analysis. Also using designing methodologies that

address the needs of the elderly and people with disabilities into strategies

of companies. 4. Technology and Automation in Ergonomics - with the emphasis on

the influence of technological advancements on health and productivity, with a

special interest in manual operations and foundry automation, and how these technologies

can be leveraged to promote ergonomic benefits. 5. Human-Machine Interaction -

and investigation how responsibilities are distributed within human-machine

systems, the ergonomic adaptation of digital platforms for different

demographics, and the ergonomic assessment of various tech-based systems. 6.

Macroergonomics - thus the holistic application of ergonomics within

organizations, considering the quality of life for employees and the integration

of ergonomic principles into the broader framework of organizational resilience

and sustainability. 7. Social Responsibility and Ergonomics - integration of

ergonomics with social responsibility, especially research how the application

of these principles can lead to improvements of enterprises practices. 8.

Ergonomics in Design & Education - ergonomics applications across various industries,

highlighting the importance of ergonomics in educational research.
