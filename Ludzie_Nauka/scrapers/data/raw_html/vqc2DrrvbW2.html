Notka biograficzna

Adam Przybyłek is an assistant professor at Gdansk University of

Technology in Poland, where he has been working since October 2012. In

the past, he was a network consultant and instructor at the Cisco

Networking Academy. More recently, he worked as a data scientist at

Decision Sciences Company, where he developed innovative AI-powered

solutions that supported the car insurance sales process. He obtained

his Ph.D. degree in Software Engineering in 2011. He also holds a

master's degree in Information Systems. His main research interests are

in empirical software engineering with focus on agile methods, software

modularity, software evolution, and post object-oriented paradigms.

Furthermore, Adam is the founder of the International Conference on Lean

and Agile Software Development (https://lasd.pl), which was

subsequently converted into a track under the same name in 2023. He is

also a co-founder of the Workshop on Personalization and Recommender

Systems (https://pers.lasd.pl). Additionally, Adam has held the position

of Program Chair for KKIO'23 (https://kkio.lasd.pl), ACM SAC'24

(https://www.sigapp.org/sac/sac2024), and ISD'24

(https://isd2024.ug.edu.pl). Moreover, he has been an active member of

program committees for ENASE and ACM SAC since 2015, MADEISD@ADBIS since

its origin in 2019, and XP since 2023.
